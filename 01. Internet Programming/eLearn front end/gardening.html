Gardening tools and how to use them

Hand Trowel

Insert the trowel into the soil, twist to create a hole, place the plant, and cover with soil.

Pruning Shears (Secateurs)

Place the blades around the branch or stem, and make a clean cut.

Garden Fork

Insert the fork into the soil, angle it backward, and lift to loosen the soil.

Garden Rake

Drag the rake over the soil to even the surface or gather debris.

Hoe

Push or pull the hoe through the soil to remove weeds and cultivate.

Garden Gloves

Wear gloves when handling soil, plants, or using tools to avoid injuries.

Watering Can

Pour water directly onto the soil around the base of plants.

Wheelbarrow

Load materials into the wheelbarrow and push or pull to move them.

Garden Pruner

Hold the pruner close to the base of the branch and make a clean cut.

Soil pH Tester

Insert the probe into the soil, wait for results, and adjust soil amendments as needed.

Garden Kneeler and Seat

Fold it out to kneel or sit, and use the handles for support when standing up.

Loppers

Place the blades around the branch, close the handles, and make a cutting motion.

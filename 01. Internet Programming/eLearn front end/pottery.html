Learning pottery

Getting started

Materials: Gather clay, a pottery wheel (optional), sculpting tools, a water basin, and a kiln for firing. Prepare Your Workspace: Set up a clean and organized workspace. Cover surfaces to avoid clay sticking.

Basic Techniques

Wedging: Knead and prepare the clay to remove air bubbles and achieve a consistent texture. Pinching: Create forms by pinching and shaping the clay with your fingers. Coiling: Roll clay into long coils and stack them to build forms. Slab Building: Flatten clay into slabs and construct forms by layering or joining.

Using a Pottery Wheel (if available)

Centering: Learn to center the clay on the wheel to ensure even shapes. Throwing: Shape the clay on the spinning wheel using various techniques.

Hand-Building Projects

Mug: Start with a simple mug using hand-building techniques. Bowl: Create a bowl using coiling or slab-building methods. Sculpture: Experiment with sculpting clay into unique forms.

Surface Decoration

Carving: Use tools to carve designs or patterns into the clay. Glazing: Apply glazes to add color and finish to your pottery.

Firing

Bisque Firing: The first firing to harden the clay. Glaze Firing: If using glazes, fire the pottery again after applying glaze.
